Anxiety Virus




The novel coronavirus has spread extensively worldwide, transmitting not only pathogens but also negative emotions such as anxiety, loneliness, and depression. In the post-pandemic era, these negative emotions continue to spread through the internet and various channels, leading to an increasing number of individuals feeling anxious. My project draws inspiration from this phenomenon, focusing on the psychological issues triggered by the pandemic. The anxiety arising during the pandemic acts like a "virus," infecting the population and giving rise to a "virus of anxiety," which leads to more individuals experiencing psychological problems.

Inspiration


As the COVID-19 virus spread extensively across the globe, it transmitted more than just pathogens; it also disseminated feelings of anxiety, loneliness, depression, and other negative emotions. My project takes its inspiration from the concept of "infection" and centers around the psychological issues triggered by the pandemic.

Project Introduction


In the current post-pandemic era, we are witnessing a growing impact of anxiety stemming from the pandemic, with this anxiety phenomenon spreading through networks among the population, resembling a "virus of anxiety."

As a response, I am committed to merging therapy with artistic expression to aid individuals in addressing anxiety triggered by the pandemic. The aim is to empower them to confront this "anxiety virus" with resilience, ultimately rendering them immune to its effects.

My Work



My project combines exposure therapy with art therapy techniques, utilizing camera and sound interaction technology to create an interactive installation.







This installation generates images based on facial and voice recognition, visualizing people's anxiety emotions and presenting an image of the "anxiety virus." Through interaction, individuals can relax and alleviate anxiety, experiencing psychological healing.







Here are the anxiety virus images from four different participants






In the exhibition, the project is divided into two parts: computer interaction and graphic design interaction, along with a multi-person interactive element. This multi-layered interactive approach aims to provide the audience with a more intuitive understanding of the project's theme and emotions.








Through this project, I also want to convey the message that, in the face of an unknown disease, we should not succumb to anxiety or avoidance. Instead, we need to bravely confront and resolve it. This project visualizes the virus model through images, helping people confront the virus without anxiety or fear. Through experiencing the project, individuals will encounter beautiful visual scenes that can heal their inner selves.
